# Module 01 — Introduction to Information Security

## Overview
This module covers the foundational concepts of information security — 
what we're actually protecting, and why.

## Key Concepts

### The CIA Triad
The core of almost every security decision comes down to balancing three things:
- **Confidentiality** — making sure data is only accessible to those authorized to see it
- **Integrity** — making sure data stays accurate and isn't tampered with
- **Availability** — making sure data/systems are accessible when actually needed

These three often pull against each other. Lock something down too hard for 
confidentiality, and you can end up hurting availability. Most security 
trade-offs come back to balancing this triangle.

### Threats vs Vulnerabilities vs Risk
- **Threat** — anything that could cause harm (an attacker, malware, natural disaster)
- **Vulnerability** — a weakness that could be exploited
- **Risk** — the likelihood and impact of a threat actually exploiting a vulnerability

## What Clicked For Me
Security isn't about building an unbreakable wall — it's about managing trade-offs. 
Every decision (encryption, access control, backups) shifts the balance between 
confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
