# Introduction to the Threat Landscape 3.0 — Fortinet Training Institute

## Overview
This course covers the foundational threat landscape: who's behind attacks, 
how attacks actually happen, and how organizations track and respond to 
threats. First course completed toward Fortinet's NSE 1 certification.

## Key Concepts

### Bad Actor Types
Different categories of threat actors, each with different motivations and 
capabilities:
- **Script kiddies** — low skill, use existing tools, often for notoriety
- **Hacktivists** — politically/socially motivated
- **Organized cybercriminals** — financially motivated, often well-resourced
- **Nation-state actors** — highly resourced, espionage/sabotage motivated
- **Insider threats** — people who already have legitimate access

### Types of Cyber Attacks & Attack Vectors
- **Attack vector** = the path or method used to gain unauthorized access
- Common vectors: phishing/email, malware, unpatched vulnerabilities, weak 
  credentials, social engineering
- The type of attack often depends on the actor's goal (data theft, disruption, 
  financial gain, espionage)

### Threat Intelligence
- The process of collecting, analyzing, and using information about threats 
  to make better security decisions
- Raw data vs. actionable intelligence — intel is only useful once it's 
  analyzed and applied to a specific context
- Organizations use threat intel to anticipate attacks, not just react to them

### Real-World Example: FortiGuard Outbreak Alerts
Fortinet's threat intel team publishes alerts when a major attack is actively 
happening in the wild (e.g. a ransomware campaign or an actively-exploited 
zero-day). This is threat intelligence in practice — turning raw observation 
of an attack into something defenders can act on.

## What Clicked For Me
Security isn't about building an unbreakable wall — it's about managing 
trade-offs. Understanding *who* is attacking (actor type) and *how* they're 
likely to do it (attack vector) is what makes threat intelligence useful — 
it lets defenders anticipate instead of just react.
